Ingredients

  • Roma Tomatoes (1kg)
  • Tomato Paste (1 can)
  • Whole Milk (1 cup)
  • Roma Tomatoes (1kg)
  • Olive Oil (2Tbsp)
  • Onion (1 large)
  • Garlic (3 cloves)
  • Parsley (1 cup)
  • Bay Leaves (3-4)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Chili Peppers (to taste)
  • Sausages (1 per person)

*** Optional Ingredients ***

  • Mushrooms (1 cup)
  • Bell Peppers (1 cup)

Necessary Equipment

  • Vegetable Mill
  • Large Wooden Spoon
  • Two Large Saucepots
  • Cutting Board
  • Sharp Knife

Directions

Step 1

Getting Ready...

Take out two large sauce pots and fill one about halfway with water. Set them both on the stove and turn the element up to High underneath the one with water to get it to a rolling boil. While the water is getting up to temperature, place your vegetable mill on top of the other saucepot and start grinding the tomatoes. Once you've finished milling all the tomatoes, the water should be up to temp. Turn the element under the pot of boiling water to Medium-High heat and throw the sausages in. Keep the sausages in there for around 7-10 minutes (varies with every stove-top), remove them from the pot, cut them in half and set them aside in a bowl. You can get rid of the water pot and start finishing the rest of the prep.

Step 2

Add in the Spices...

Roughly dice the Parsley and add it into the pot. Crush the Garlic and grate the onion into the pot. Add the salt, olive oil, chili pepper and bay leaves. If you've decided to add the mushrooms and bell peppers, can chop those up and add them in too.

Step 3

Almost done...

Turn the element on to Medium-Low. Add in the can of tomato paste and mix in the cup of milk. Now is a good time to grab those sausages you blanched earlier and put them in the sauce. They will finish cooking in the sauce and end up really nice.

Step 4

Finishing up...

Let the sauce stew on Medium-Low for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally. I recommend letting it cook and reduce for at the minimum 2-3 hours for best results. Can be served right away over the pasta of your choice. Enjoy!
